01

Overview

🌩️

The Weather Alert Tool is an internal NWS Melbourne browser-based application for building, visualizing, and exporting storm impact graphics in real time. It combines live radar and satellite imagery, NWS warning polygons, interactive storm tracking, and freehand annotation into a single map interface built for operational use during severe weather events.

The tool is designed to be used on a desktop or laptop browser (best experienced full-screen). It does not require login and is intended for internal forecasters and media liaisons.

Quick start: Open the tool, select a data layer from the DATA dropdown, and press D to start drawing or C to enter Cone tracking mode. Press E to export when ready.
02

Interface Layout

🗺️

The interface is built around a full-screen map. All controls are overlaid on top of the map canvas and will dim after 7 seconds of inactivity — hover over any control to restore it to full brightness.

Toolbar (Top-Left)

The primary toolbar contains two rows of grouped buttons:

  • Row 1 — Mode Controls: PAN, HOME 🏠, CONE, TRACK, DRAW, SPOT, ID ROADS, ERASE, TEXT, EXPORT, HELP
  • Row 2 — Context Controls: DATA selector, Opacity (◐), Duration (30m / 1h / 2h), Fan width slider, CLEAR TRACK, WARNINGS, ⚡ Lightning

Help Hint (Top-Right)

A small panel in the top-right corner dynamically shows instructions for the currently active mode. Watch this to confirm you're in the correct mode.

Storm Impact Card (Bottom-Left)

When a storm track is active, a draggable card appears showing the computed storm speed in MPH and a list of cities within the projected impact path. Each city row includes the estimated time of arrival (ETA). You can hover over a city row to reveal a remove button, excluding it from the impact list.

Data Legend (Bottom-Right)

When MRMS products are selected (such as QPE or Hail Size), a color legend appears in the bottom-right corner identifying the data thresholds.

03

Toolbar Modes

🎛️

The tool operates in one mode at a time. The active mode is highlighted in the toolbar. Press Esc at any time to return to Pan mode.

PAN P

Pan Mode

Default navigation mode. Click and drag to move the map. Scroll to zoom in and out. Hold CTRL + click and drag to tilt and rotate map in 3D. No drawing or tracking is active.

🏠

Home Button

Instantly flies the map back to the default view centered on Central Florida (28.5°N, 81.4°W) at zoom level 7, and returns to Pan mode.

CONE C

Cone Mode

Click and drag from the storm's current location toward its projected end point. A cone of uncertainty polygon is drawn with a centerline and tick marks. The Storm Impact Card shows computed speed and city ETAs. Fan width is adjustable via the FAN slider. Releases to Pan mode automatically.

TRACK L

Manual Track Mode

A two-step tracker: Step 1 — click and drag to draw the leading edge of the storm cell. Step 2 — release, then move the mouse to project the track box forward in real time. Step 3 — click anywhere to lock the projection into place. Click again to unlock and return to Pan.

DRAW D

Draw Mode

Freehand annotation tool. Click and drag to draw smooth, Bezier-curved lines on the map in the selected color and width. Multiple strokes accumulate — use ERASE (Shift+C) to clear all drawings at once.

SPOT S

Spotlight Mode

Click and drag to draw a closed shape around an area of interest. The rest of the map is dimmed with a semi-transparent black overlay, focusing attention on the highlighted area. Useful for directing attention to a specific county or storm cell.

ID ROADS R

Road ID Mode

Click on any major road to stamp a persistent label at that location showing the road name (e.g. I-4, US-1, FL TPKE). Hover to preview the name before clicking. Labels accumulate until cleared with ERASE. Florida-specific road aliases are applied automatically.

TEXT T

Text Box

Opens a draggable, editable text box on the map. Supports bold, italic, text color, background color, font size (Small → Huge), and alignment (Left / Center / Right). Drag the handle bar to reposition. Close with the red X button.

ERASE Shift+C

Erase All

Immediately clears all freehand drawings, spotlight overlays, road ID labels, and data sample stamps from the map. Does not remove the storm track — use CLEAR TRACK for that.

CLEAR TRACK C

Clear Track

Removes the active storm track polygon, centerline, tick marks, ETA labels, and the Storm Impact Card. Resets to Pan mode. Note: this does not clear freehand drawings — use ERASE for those.

Note on Shift+C vs C: Pressing C alone clears the active storm track. Pressing Shift+C clears all freehand drawings and annotations. These are two separate operations.

Opacity Control

Click the ◐ icon next to the DATA selector to open a mini panel. The opacity slider controls transparency for all active data layers simultaneously. Default is 90%. Useful for balancing radar visibility against the base map when presenting.

Data Threshold Filter

When an MRMS product is active (QPE, MESH, Regional Reflectivity), a Data Threshold slider appears inside the opacity panel. Slide it to filter out values below a given threshold — helpful for suppressing light rain noise when focusing on heavy rainfall or hail cores.

Data Sampling (Eyedropper)

When an MRMS product is loaded, a 💧 eyedropper icon appears in the DATA group. Click it to activate sample mode, then click anywhere on the data overlay to stamp the measured value at that point (reflectivity in dBZ, precipitation in inches, hail size in inches). Samples persist until cleared with ERASE.

Lightning Overlay

Click the ⚡ button to toggle real-time lightning strike data from MRMS. When active, bolt icons appear on the map and the data refreshes automatically every 60 seconds. Lightning data is sourced from jhwxtools.com/mrms.

Auto-refresh: NWS warning polygons update automatically every 2 minutes. Lightning data refreshes every 60 seconds when the lightning layer is active.
05

Storm Tracking

🌀

Duration Selection

Before drawing a track, select a forecast duration using the 30m 1h 2h buttons that appear in Row 2 when Cone or Track mode is active. This determines how far forward the track is projected. Default is 1 hour.

Cone Mode — Step by Step

  1. Select a data layer so the storm is visible.
  2. Press C or click CONE to enter Cone mode.
  3. Choose the forecast duration (30m, 1h, or 2h).
  4. Optionally adjust the FAN slider to set cone width (20–50°).
  5. Click on the storm's current location and hold the mouse button down.
  6. Drag toward the projected end point. The cone, centerline, and speed display update in real time as you drag.
  7. Release the mouse. The tool automatically returns to Pan mode. The Storm Impact Card appears showing city ETAs.

Manual Track Mode — Step by Step

  1. Press L or click TRACK to enter Manual Track mode.
  2. Choose the forecast duration (30m, 1h, or 2h).
  3. Click and drag to draw the leading edge of the storm. This is the front line of the storm cell — draw it perpendicular to the storm's direction of motion.
  4. Release the mouse. The edge is now locked.
  5. Move the mouse away from the edge in the direction of storm motion. A track box and projection automatically follow your cursor, showing speed and ETAs live.
  6. Click to lock the projection in place. The track is finalized and the tool returns to Pan mode.
  7. Click anywhere on the map to unlock and reset.

Storm Impact Card

Once a track is drawn, the Storm Impact Card appears in the lower-left corner. It shows:

  • Storm Speed — Computed from the drag distance and selected duration, displayed in MPH.
  • City Impact List — Cities within the projected impact area, sorted by ETA. Hover to reveal a Remove button to exclude a city from the list. This will also hide it from the track ETA pop-ups on the map.

The card can be dragged anywhere on the screen by its header handle. It is automatically hidden during export.

ETA Tick Marks

Time tick marks are drawn along the centerline at regular intervals based on storm speed. These show how long it will take the storm to reach each point along the track. Tick marks are for 15 minute intervals on 30 minute and one hour tracks and 30 minutes for 2 hour tracks.

Starting a new track: Beginning a new drag in Cone or Track mode will automatically clear the previous track. You do not need to press Clear Track first.
06

Annotations

✏️

Freehand Drawing

Press D or click DRAW. Click and drag to draw smooth freehand lines. Strokes are Bezier-smoothed on release. Each stroke is a separate object — multiple strokes accumulate on the map until you use ERASE.

The default draw color is yellow. To change color, you currently need to use the draw color variable in the source. Width defaults to 6px.

Spotlight Overlay

Press S or click SPOT. Click and drag to draw a closed freehand boundary around the area you want to highlight. When you release, everything outside the boundary is darkened by a semi-transparent black overlay, drawing the viewer's eye to the interior region. Use it to focus attention on a specific county or cell during a briefing.

Road ID Labels

Press R or click ID ROADS. Hover over major roads — a yellow tooltip previews the road name. Click to stamp a persistent label at that location. Florida-specific aliases are applied automatically (e.g. FL-5 becomes US-1, FL-91 becomes FL TPKE). Labels persist until ERASE is used.

Text Box

Press T or click TEXT to open a floating, editable text panel. You can:

  • Type or paste any text into the editable area.
  • Change text color and background color via the color pickers.
  • Set font size: Small, Normal, Large, X-Large, or Huge.
  • Apply alignment: Left (L), Center (C), Right (R).
  • Toggle Bold (B) or Italic (I).
  • Drag the handle bar at the top to reposition the box. Close with the red X button.
Tip: The text box is ideal for adding event titles, timestamps, or area descriptions to your exported graphic.

Clearing Annotations

Press Shift+C or click ERASE to clear all freehand drawings, spotlights, road ID labels, and data sample stamps at once. The storm track is unaffected — use CLEAR TRACK (C) for that.

NWS Warnings

🚨

Press W or click WARNINGS to toggle the active NWS warning polygon layer. Warning polygons are color-coded by hazard type and refresh automatically every 2 minutes.

Clicking a Warning Polygon

  1. With warnings visible, click on any colored warning polygon. Note: warnings cannot be queried if road ID or sample mode are enabled.
  2. The map auto-fits to the warning's bounding box (if zoomed out).
  3. The selected warning is highlighted at full opacity; all others dim.
  4. A popup appears showing the warning type, expiration time, hazard details (wind speed, hail size, tornado, water rescues), motion direction and speed, and affected area description.
  5. Click the map background to dismiss the popup and reset highlighting.

Warning Popup Features

  • Editable fields — Click on any text in the popup (event name, expiration, hazards, affected area) to edit it inline before exporting.
  • Hail descriptions — Hail size is automatically translated to a size reference (e.g. "1.75 in (Golf Ball)").
  • Motion — Storm motion is parsed from the NWS warning data and displayed as cardinal direction and rounded MPH.
  • Draggable — The popup can be dragged by its header to reposition it anywhere on screen.
  • Social Media Text — Hover over the pop-up and click COPY SHORT TEXT to get a sub-240 character snippet to copy for social media.
Storm track interaction: When a storm track is active, warning polygons dim to 20% opacity so the track is visible. The selected (clicked) warning always shows at full opacity regardless.

Export

📤

The export system captures the current map view as a 16:9 graphic (1350×759 px) with an NWS Melbourne branded banner.

STEP 01
Set up your map Pan, zoom, and layer your data before exporting.
STEP 02
Press Export Press E or click EXPORT.
STEP 03
Enter Password Required once per session. Use the password found at this link..
STEP 04
Frame the shot The viewfinder overlay appears. Pan and zoom to position the content inside the frame. Move warning pop-up if needed.
STEP 05
Confirm or cancel Press Enter or click EXPORT NOW & COPY. Press Esc or click Cancel to exit without exporting.

What Gets Captured

  • The map view within the blue viewfinder border (1350×639 px map area).
  • All active data layers, storm tracks, freehand drawings, spotlight overlays, road labels, and data samples.
  • A 120px header banner reading WEATHER IMPACT · UPDATE with a timestamp and the NWS Melbourne WFO logo.

What Is Hidden During Export

  • The toolbar and all UI controls.
  • The Storm Impact Card (city list). Plan to communicate impact separately or include a custom text box on the map.

After Export

The graphic is rendered and automatically copied to your clipboard as a PNG image. Paste it directly into Slack, email, or any social media tool. The tool stays open and all annotations remain intact after export.

Best practice: Add key details (storm name, time, affected areas) using the TEXT tool before exporting. Position the text box inside the viewfinder frame for it to appear in the final image.

Tips & Recommended Workflow

💡

Recommended Severe Weather Briefing Workflow

  1. Select radar product. Pick MLB REF for a single-site view, or MRMS for the full Florida picture. Adjust opacity to ~80% for legibility.
  2. Annotate key roads. Press R and click on at-risk corridors to stamp persistent road labels. Turn off Road ID mode when done sampling.
  3. Sample values (if MRMS). Press 💧 and click to sample QPE, hail, or reflectivity values. Turn off sample mode when done sampling.
  4. Enable warnings. Press W to toggle warning polygons on. Click a polygon to see its full details and motion data.
  5. Draw the storm track. Use CONE for a standard forecast cone or TRACK for a precise linear storm box. Select an appropriate duration first.
  6. Add spotlight if needed. Press S and draw around the county of concern to focus viewer attention.
  7. Add a text callout. Press T to open a text box for storm title, time, or impacted area summary.
  8. Export. Press E, frame the shot, and press Enter to copy to clipboard.

General Tips

Annotating: You can draw annotations with DRAW mode on top of a cone to highlight specific areas, roads, or cities within the impact corridor.
Clicking On Items: Your highlighted warning may get cleared if trying to draw a track or cone. If that occurs, click outside of any warning, then click the warning of interest again.
Sampling QPE: Switch to a QPE product, activate the eyedropper (💧), and click on flooded areas to stamp rainfall totals directly on the map before exporting.
Toolbar fades: The toolbar dims after 7 seconds. Simply hover over it to bring it back to full brightness — this keeps your map view clean during screen shares.
Annotations are not saved. All drawings, tracks, labels, and text are held in memory only. Closing or refreshing the browser will clear everything. Export before closing.
